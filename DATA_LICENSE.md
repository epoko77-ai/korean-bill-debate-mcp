# Parliamentary record notice

The Apache-2.0 license covers the source code, not parliamentary records.

Metadata, short review excerpts, and derived indexes remain subject to the terms published by
their official Korean National Assembly sources. KASM preserves official source URLs and
content hashes so users can verify records and determine the applicable terms. The repository
does not redistribute the full minutes corpus or raw API cache.

The National Assembly copyright policy states that works explicitly marked KOGL Type 1 may be
reused with source attribution, while use of unmarked materials should be discussed with the
responsible office in advance. Review fixtures therefore contain only minimal quotations needed
to test speaker-boundary parsing and attribute every excerpt to its exact official PDF. See:
https://assembly.go.kr/portal/main/contents.do?menuNo=600250

Before redistributing a prepared database or vector index, operators must review the current
Open Assembly terms and any attribution or redistribution requirements. Contributors should
commit only the minimum excerpt needed for a reproducible parser test.
